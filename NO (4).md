## STATEMENT OF THE CASE

In Case No. 1:25-cv-04130, Petitioner sought emergency injunctive relief and the appointment of a Special Master with expertise in cryptography and genetic analysis to authenticate his neurogenetic evidence. Despite Petitioner possessing $0 income and $0 assets due to disability-related unemployment, the District Court dismissed the complaint while an IFP motion was pending, citing "fee non-payment."

This dismissal is part of a "ping-pong" trap where the federal court refuses to vacate its dismissal despite the state court proceeding (D-01-CR-25-011146) resulting in *nolle prosequi*. By denying the Gov-LLM "cognitive ramp," the court has imposed a functional "Restraint of Silence," barring Petitioner from meaningful comprehension or participation in his own defense, an injury that is "effectively unreviewable" after final judgment.

---

## REASONS FOR GRANTING THE WRIT

### I. The Collateral Order Doctrine and Structural Barriers
The denial of a cognitive interface is not a mere procedural hiccup; it is a final determination of Petitioner's right to access the court. Under *Cohen*, this order is collateral because it is (1) conclusive, (2) resolves an important issue separate from the merits, and (3) is effectively unreviewable on appeal from a final judgment.

### II. Constructive Custody via Cognitive Segregation
Under *Olmstead*, the unjustified isolation of individuals with disabilities is a form of discrimination. By barring the only technological tool that allows Petitioner to overcome his neurogenetic "biological tax," the court has placed him in a state of constructive custody, where his silence is compelled by his biology and enforced by the court's refusal to accommodate.
