- **Litigation Trend:** Significant increase in cases challenging employer reliance on mitigating measures
- **Caustin Relevance:** Genomic risk scores provide objective, non-mitigating documentation of functional impairment

## III. SECTION 504 COMPLIANCE & VIOLATIONS

### A. Federal Enforcement Data
- **Statute:** Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. § 794)
- **Scope:** Federally funded programs (education, healthcare, social services)
- **Enforcement:** U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights (OCR)

### B. OCR Complaint Statistics (Estimated)
- **Annual Complaints:** 5,000-7,000 (across all sectors)
- **Education Sector:** ~60% of complaints
- **Common Violations:**
  - Failure to provide reasonable accommodations
  - Discriminatory discipline of students with disabilities
  - Failure to conduct Section 504 evaluations
  - Inadequate accessibility (physical and digital)

### C. Digital Accessibility Violations (Emerging Trend)
- **Statute:** Section 504 applies to digital platforms of federally funded entities
- **Standard:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA (DOJ guidance)
- **Trend:** Significant increase in digital accessibility complaints (2020-2026)
- **Settlements:** Average $50,000-$500,000 per case
- **Caustin Implication:** Accessible technology development under Section 504 mandate
