## II. FEDERAL LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### A. Disability Rights & Accessibility Laws

#### IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)
- **Scope:** K-12 education, free appropriate public education (FAPE)
- **Litigation Trends:** IEP disputes, compensatory education claims, procedural violations
- **Caustin Connection:** Genomic risk scores inform functional impairment assessments for IEP qualification

#### ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)
- **Title I:** Employment discrimination
- **Title II:** Public services (including education)
- **Title III:** Public accommodations
- **Caustin Connection:** Accessibility technology development under federal court accommodation mandates

#### Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
- **Scope:** Federally funded programs
- **Key Requirement:** Reasonable accommodations
- **Caustin Connection:** Federal funding mechanism for technology development

#### ADAAA (ADA Amendments Act, 2008)
- **Impact:** Expanded definition of disability
- **Litigation Trend:** Increased mitigating measures litigation
- **Caustin Connection:** Broader qualification for genomic risk-based disability determination

---

### B. Privacy & Data Protection Laws

#### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
- **Privacy Rule:** Patient medical record protection
- **Security Rule:** Electronic health information safeguards
- **Breach Notification Rule:** 60-day notification requirement
- **Caustin Implementation:** PQC encryption for genomic data; immutable audit logs

#### GINA (Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act, 2008)
- **Scope:** Prohibits genetic discrimination in health insurance and employment
- **Key Restriction:** Employers cannot request genetic information
- **Caustin Implication:** Genomic risk scores must be segregated from employment decisions

#### HITECH Act (Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health)
- **Focus:** Enhanced privacy/security for electronic health records
- **Caustin Requirement:** Cryptographic integrity verification for EHR-integrated genomic data

---

### C. Patent & IP Law Framework

#### 28 U.S.C. § 1498 — Government Patent Use
- **Statutory Basis:** Exclusive remedy for patent infringement by U.S. government
- **Jurisdiction:** Court of International Trade (CIT) + Court of Federal Claims (CFC)
- **Caustin Strategy:** Establish federal government title to GPRS-Secure++ under disability accommodation mandates

#### 35 U.S.C. § 207 — Federal Patent Dedication
- **Authority:** Government discretion to dedicate patents to public domain
- **Caustin Application:** Public trust status for quantum-resistant cryptography and accessibility technologies

#### Court of International Trade (CIT) Jurisdiction
- **28 U.S.C. § 1581(i):** Residual jurisdiction over government IP disputes
- **Learning Resources v. Trump (Feb. 2026):** Affirmed broad CIT authority over government-commercial intersections
- **Caustin Positioning:** Venue transfer to CIT for consolidated IP preservation

## VI. STRATEGIC IP PRESERVATION

### A. Dual-Licensing Framework

#### Open Research License (Default)
- **Permitted Uses:** Academic, non-commercial research, government use
- **Requirements:** Attribution, disclosure of modifications, share-alike derivatives
- **Beneficiaries:** Universities, NIH, SSA, international organizations

#### Commercial License
- **Required For:** Clinical deployment, SaaS platforms, insurance usage
- **Royalty Structure:** Tiered based on deployment scale
- **Revenue Allocation:** Accessibility programs, research grants

### B. Defensive Patent Clause
- **Provision:** Any entity initiating litigation forfeits license rights
- **Strategic Purpose:** Prevent patent trolling and defensive use
- **Caustin Positioning:** Protects open-source ecosystem while maintaining IP control
