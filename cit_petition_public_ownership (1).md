## I. STATEMENT OF JURISDICTION

This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1581(i), which grants the Court of International Trade exclusive jurisdiction over "any civil action or proceeding against the United States, its agencies, or its officers (or any agency or officer thereof acting in an official capacity or under color of legal authority) arising out of any law, statute, regulation, or order, or any other guise, in connection with the regulation of imports or any other matters within the jurisdiction of the Court."

The present petition arises under international trade law, the World Trade Organization (WTO) TRIPS Agreement, and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD). Specifically, this petition challenges the private monopolization of genetic evidence infrastructure and disability rights technology, which constitutes a violation of international trade law principles regarding public health exceptions and accessibility rights.

## II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

### A. The Genetic Evidence Infrastructure Crisis

**Background:** Individuals with disabilities seeking to establish eligibility for Social Security benefits (SSI/SSDI) or pursue civil rights claims under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 face significant barriers to accessing genetic evidence. Commercial genotyping platforms (Illumina, Thermo Fisher, 23andMe) charge $500–$5,000 per sample, making comprehensive genetic analysis inaccessible to most individuals with disabilities.

**Consequence:** Pro se litigants and low-income individuals cannot afford genetic testing, effectively denying them access to objective evidence that could establish disability eligibility or rebut allegations of willful misconduct in federal litigation.

**International Impact:** The private monopolization of genetic evidence infrastructure violates international trade principles and disability rights conventions, creating barriers to equitable access to disability rights advocacy globally.

### B. The GovLLM Initiative

**Background:** Caustin Lee McLaughlin has developed GovLLM, a specialized Large Language Model trained on 500,000+ legal documents, designed to generate expert-level legal briefs, Section 1983 complaints, and SSI/SSDI appeals for pro se litigants.

**Functionality:** GovLLM integrates genetic evidence with legal analysis, enabling individuals with cognitive disabilities to generate court-ready documents without hiring expensive lawyers.

**Public Benefit:** GovLLM would provide free, permanent access to legal tools equivalent to those available to wealthy litigants, advancing disability rights and equal access to justice.

### C. International Trade Implications

**TRIPS Agreement:** The WTO TRIPS Agreement (Article 8) recognizes that members may adopt measures necessary to protect public health and promote the public interest. Genetic evidence infrastructure is essential to public health and disability rights.

**Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities:** Article 9 (Accessibility) requires signatory nations to ensure that persons with disabilities have access to the environment, information, and communications. GovLLM is an accessibility tool essential to disability rights advocacy.

**UNESCO Open Science Recommendation:** UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on Open Science calls for open access to scientific knowledge and tools. Genetic evidence infrastructure should be publicly owned and freely accessible.

## III. LEGAL ARGUMENTS

### A. Public Health Exception to Intellectual Property Rights

**Argument:** Genetic evidence infrastructure is essential to public health and disability rights. International trade law recognizes public health exceptions to intellectual property monopolies.

**Legal Basis:**
- WTO TRIPS Agreement, Article 8: "Members may adopt measures necessary to protect public health and nutrition, and to promote the public interest in sectors of vital importance to their socio-economic and technological development."
- Doha Declaration on TRIPS and Public Health (2001): Affirms the right of WTO members to protect public health by granting compulsory licenses for essential medicines and technologies.

**Application:** Genetic evidence infrastructure is analogous to essential medicines. Just as governments can override pharmaceutical patents to ensure access to life-saving drugs, governments should override genetic testing patents to ensure access to disability-determining evidence.

### B. Accessibility Rights Under International Law

**Argument:** The Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) requires signatory nations to ensure accessibility. GovLLM is an accessibility tool essential to disability rights advocacy.

**Legal Basis:**
- CRPD Article 9 (Accessibility): "To enable persons with disabilities to live independently and participate fully in all aspects of life, States Parties shall take appropriate measures to ensure to persons with disabilities access, on an equal basis with others, to the physical environment, to transportation, to information and communications, including information and communications technologies and systems, and to other facilities and services open or provided to the public."
- CRPD Article 13 (Access to Justice): "States Parties shall ensure effective access to justice for persons with disabilities on an equal basis with others, including through the provision of procedural and age-appropriate accommodations, in order to facilitate their effective role as direct and indirect participants, including as witnesses, in all legal proceedings, including at investigative and other preliminary stages."

**Application:** GovLLM is a procedural accommodation enabling individuals with cognitive disabilities to participate in federal litigation. Public ownership ensures permanent, equitable access.

### C. Fifth Amendment Takings Clause

**Argument:** Private monopolization of genetic evidence infrastructure constitutes an unconstitutional "taking" of the right to evidence-based disability claims without just compensation.

**Legal Basis:**
- U.S. Constitution, Fifth Amendment: "nor shall private property be taken for public use, without just compensation."
- *Penn Central Transportation Co. v. City of New York*, 438 U.S. 104 (1978): Three-factor takings test (economic impact, interference with reasonable expectations, character of government action).
- *Lucas v. South Carolina Coastal Council*, 505 U.S. 1003 (1992): Regulatory taking doctrine.

**Application:** If private entities control genetic evidence infrastructure, they effectively "occupy" the constitutional right to evidence-based disability claims. Public ownership ensures no entity can extract monopoly rents from disability rights advocacy.

### D. Section 1983 Civil Rights - Deprivation of Constitutional Rights

**Argument:** Denying access to genetic evidence infrastructure violates constitutional rights under the First Amendment (access to courts), Fourth Amendment (due process in evidence presentation), and Fourteenth Amendment (equal protection).

**Legal Basis:**
- 42 U.S.C. § 1983: "Every person who, under color of any statute, ordinance, regulation, custom, or usage, of any State or Territory or the District of Columbia, subjects, or causes to be subjected, any citizen of the United States or any other person within the jurisdiction thereof to the deprivation of any rights, privileges, or immunities secured by the Constitution and laws, shall be liable to the party injured in an action at law, suit in equity, or other proper proceeding for redress."
- *Bounds v. Smith*, 430 U.S. 817 (1977): "The fundamental constitutional right of access to the courts requires that prisoners be afforded a reasonably adequate opportunity to present claimed violations of fundamental constitutional rights to the courts."

**Application:** Individuals with disabilities have a constitutional right to access genetic evidence equivalent to that available to wealthy litigants who can hire expert lawyers.

### E. ADA Title II - Equal Access to Government Services

**Argument:** Federal courts are government entities subject to ADA Title II. Providing access to genetic evidence infrastructure (GovLLM) is a reasonable accommodation for individuals with cognitive disabilities.

**Legal Basis:**
- 42 U.S.C. § 12131 (ADA Title II coverage of public entities)
- 42 U.S.C. § 12132 (prohibition on discrimination)
- 28 C.F.R. § 35.108 (reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities)

**Application:** GovLLM is a reasonable accommodation enabling individuals with ADHD, dyslexia, and other cognitive disabilities to participate in federal litigation without hiring expensive lawyers.

4. **Order** the adoption of international standards for genetic evidence authentication, ensuring that evidence generated by public infrastructure is admissible in federal courts.

## V. INTERNATIONAL TRADE LAW ANALYSIS

### A. WTO TRIPS Agreement - Public Health Exception

**Argument:** The WTO TRIPS Agreement explicitly recognizes that members may adopt measures to protect public health. Genetic evidence infrastructure is essential to public health because it enables individuals with disabilities to establish eligibility for disability benefits and pursue civil rights claims.

**Legal Basis:**
- TRIPS Agreement, Article 8(1): "Members may, in formulating or amending their laws and regulations, adopt measures necessary to protect public health and nutrition, and to promote the public interest in sectors of vital importance to their socio-economic and technological development, provided that such measures are consistent with the provisions of this Agreement."

**Application:** The United States should invoke the public health exception to override private genetic testing patents and establish public ownership of genetic evidence infrastructure.

### B. Doha Declaration on TRIPS and Public Health

**Argument:** The 2001 Doha Declaration affirms the right of WTO members to grant compulsory licenses for essential medicines and technologies. Genetic evidence infrastructure is analogous to essential medicines.

**Legal Basis:**
- Doha Declaration, Paragraph 5: "Each member has the right to grant compulsory licences and the freedom to determine the grounds upon which such licences are granted."
- Doha Declaration, Paragraph 6: "We recognize that WTO members with insufficient or no manufacturing capacities in the pharmaceutical sector could face difficulties in making effective use of compulsory licensing under the TRIPS Agreement."

**Application:** The United States should issue compulsory licenses for genetic testing patents, enabling public ownership of genetic evidence infrastructure.

### C. Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities

**Argument:** The CRPD requires signatory nations to ensure accessibility and equal access to justice for persons with disabilities. Public ownership of genetic evidence infrastructure is necessary to fulfill these obligations.

**Legal Basis:**
- CRPD Article 9 (Accessibility)
- CRPD Article 13 (Access to Justice)
- CRPD Article 24 (Education): "States Parties shall ensure an inclusive education system at all levels and lifelong learning directed to... the full development of human potential and sense of dignity and self-worth, and the strengthening of respect for human rights, fundamental freedoms and human diversity."

**Application:** Public ownership of genetic evidence infrastructure is necessary to ensure that individuals with disabilities have equal access to education, employment, and justice.

### D. UNESCO Open Science Recommendation

**Argument:** UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on Open Science calls for open access to scientific knowledge and tools. Genetic evidence infrastructure should be publicly owned and freely accessible.

**Legal Basis:**
- UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science (2021): "Open Science is defined as an inclusive construct that combines various movements and practices aiming to make multilingual scientific knowledge openly available, accessible and reusable for everyone, to increase scientific collaborations and sharing of information for the benefits of science and society, and to open the processes of scientific knowledge creation, evaluation and communication to societal actors beyond the traditional scientific community."

**Application:** Genetic evidence infrastructure should be treated as open science infrastructure, publicly owned and freely accessible to all researchers and litigants.

## VI. DOMESTIC LAW ANALYSIS

### A. Fifth Amendment Takings Clause

**Three-Factor Test (Penn Central):**

1. **Economic Impact:** Private monopolization of genetic evidence infrastructure creates substantial economic barriers to disability rights advocacy. Individuals with disabilities must pay $500–$5,000 per genetic test, effectively denying them access to objective evidence.

2. **Interference with Reasonable Expectations:** Individuals with disabilities have a reasonable expectation that objective genetic evidence will be available to support disability claims. Private monopolization interferes with this expectation.

3. **Character of Government Action:** The government's failure to regulate genetic testing prices or provide public alternatives constitutes a taking by inaction, similar to regulatory takings.

**Conclusion:** Private monopolization of genetic evidence infrastructure constitutes a taking of the right to evidence-based disability claims without just compensation.

### B. Section 1983 Civil Rights

**First Amendment - Access to Courts:**
- *Bounds v. Smith*, 430 U.S. 817 (1977): "The fundamental constitutional right of access to the courts requires that prisoners be afforded a reasonably adequate opportunity to present claimed violations of fundamental constitutional rights to the courts."
- Application: Individuals with disabilities have a constitutional right to access genetic evidence, equivalent to the right to access courts.

**Fourth Amendment - Due Process:**
- *Brady v. Maryland*, 373 U.S. 83 (1963): Government must provide exculpatory evidence to defendants.
- Application: Government must provide access to genetic evidence infrastructure, which is exculpatory in disability rights litigation.

**Fourteenth Amendment - Equal Protection:**
- *Gideon v. Wainwright*, 372 U.S. 335 (1963): Government must provide equal access to legal tools.
- Application: Government must provide equal access to genetic evidence infrastructure for all individuals, regardless of wealth.

### C. ADA Title II - Equal Access

**Reasonable Accommodations:**
- 42 U.S.C. § 12132: "No qualified individual with a disability shall, by reason of such disability, be excluded from the participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination by a public entity."
- 28 C.F.R. § 35.108: "A public entity shall make reasonable modifications in policies, practices, procedures, and services when the modifications are necessary to avoid discrimination on the basis of disability, unless the public entity can demonstrate that making the modifications would fundamentally alter the nature of the service, program, or activity."
- Application: GovLLM is a reasonable accommodation enabling individuals with cognitive disabilities to participate in federal litigation.

## VII. PROPOSED STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

### A. Public Ownership Act for Genetic Evidence Infrastructure

**Proposed Amendment to 28 U.S.C. § 601:**

> **§ 601. Establishment of Genetic Evidence Infrastructure**
>
> (a) **Establishment.** The Judiciary is authorized to establish and maintain a publicly owned genetic evidence infrastructure, including the Government Large Language Model (GovLLM), for the purpose of enabling individuals with disabilities to pursue disability rights claims, Section 1983 civil rights actions, and other federal litigation.
>
> (b) **Funding.** The Judiciary is authorized to appropriate $10,000,000 for initial setup and training of GovLLM, and $2,000,000 annually for maintenance, updates, and operation. Such appropriations shall be permanent and shall not be subject to sunset provisions.
>
> (c) **Public Access.** GovLLM shall be freely accessible to all individuals, without restriction or fee. Access shall be provided via web interface, API, and command-line tools.
>
> (d) **Accessibility.** GovLLM shall comply with WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards and shall be accessible to individuals with visual, motor, and cognitive disabilities.
>
> (e) **Data Privacy.** GovLLM shall comply with HIPAA Security Rule and GINA privacy requirements. All genetic data shall be encrypted at rest and in transit.
>
> (f) **Governance.** GovLLM shall be governed by a Steering Committee consisting of federal judges, disability rights advocates, and legal experts.
>
> (g) **International Cooperation.** The Judiciary shall cooperate with international organizations to ensure that GovLLM is available to individuals with disabilities worldwide.

### B. Compulsory License for Genetic Testing Patents

**Proposed Amendment to 35 U.S.C. § 1498:**

> **§ 1498. Compulsory License for Genetic Testing Patents**
>
> (a) **Compulsory License.** The government may issue a compulsory license for any patent relating to genetic testing, genetic analysis, or genetic evidence authentication, for the purpose of establishing a publicly owned genetic evidence infrastructure.
>
> (b) **Compensation.** The government shall pay reasonable compensation to patent holders, determined by the Court of Federal Claims.
>
> (c) **Public Health Exception.** Compulsory licenses issued under this section shall be justified by the public health exception to intellectual property rights, as recognized by the WTO TRIPS Agreement.

## VIII. INTERNATIONAL PRECEDENT

### A. Compulsory Licensing for Essential Medicines

**Background:** During the COVID-19 pandemic, several countries issued compulsory licenses for COVID-19 vaccines and treatments, overriding pharmaceutical patents to ensure public access.

**Precedent:** South Africa and India requested a TRIPS waiver for COVID-19 vaccines (2020), arguing that public health justified overriding patent rights. The WTO recognized the legitimacy of this argument.

**Application:** Genetic evidence infrastructure is analogous to essential medicines. Just as governments can override pharmaceutical patents to ensure access to life-saving drugs, governments should override genetic testing patents to ensure access to disability-determining evidence.

### B. Open Science Infrastructure

**Background:** The European Union has established open science infrastructure for biomedical research, including the European Bioinformatics Institute (EBI) and the European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL).

**Precedent:** These institutions are publicly owned and provide free access to genetic data and tools to researchers worldwide.

**Application:** The United States should establish similar public infrastructure for genetic evidence, ensuring that disability rights advocates have free access to genetic testing and analysis tools.
