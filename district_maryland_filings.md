## MOTION 1: MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTION
### On Genetic Evidence Admissibility and Chain-of-Custody Standards

**TO THE HONORABLE COURT:**

Plaintiff Caustin Lee McLaughlin, by and through undersigned counsel, respectfully submits this Motion for Preliminary Injunction requesting that this Court establish standards for admissibility of blockchain-anchored genetic evidence in federal litigation.

### I. STATEMENT OF FACTS

1. Plaintiff has undergone genetic testing using the USB-C Electrochemical Genotyping Platform, a novel technology that provides rapid, low-cost genetic analysis with post-quantum cryptographic authentication.

2. Genetic test results have been anchored to a permissioned Hyperledger Fabric blockchain using Dilithium digital signatures, creating an immutable, tamper-proof record of genetic evidence.

3. Defendants have indicated they may challenge the admissibility of this genetic evidence on chain-of-custody grounds, arguing that the novel technology and blockchain authentication do not meet Federal Rules of Evidence standards.

4. Plaintiff seeks to establish clear evidentiary standards for blockchain-anchored genetic evidence, ensuring that pro se litigants can introduce genetic evidence without facing arbitrary exclusion.

### II. LEGAL STANDARD FOR PRELIMINARY INJUNCTION

To obtain a preliminary injunction, plaintiff must demonstrate: (1) substantial likelihood of success on the merits; (2) substantial threat of irreparable harm if injunction is not granted; (3) balance of equities in plaintiff's favor; and (4) injunction is in the public interest. *Winter v. Natural Resources Defense Council*, 555 U.S. 7 (2008).

### III. ARGUMENT

#### A. Substantial Likelihood of Success on the Merits

**1. Blockchain-Anchored Evidence Meets Federal Rules of Evidence Standards**

Federal Rule of Evidence 901(a) requires that evidence be authenticated by "evidence sufficient to support a finding that the item is what the proponent claims it is." Blockchain-anchored genetic evidence satisfies this standard because:

- **Immutability:** Blockchain records are cryptographically secured and cannot be retroactively modified without detection.
- **Transparency:** All blockchain transactions are recorded and can be independently verified.
- **Chain of Custody:** Blockchain provides a complete, auditable chain of custody from sample collection to result generation.
- **Post-Quantum Security:** Dilithium signatures are resistant to quantum computing attacks, ensuring long-term evidence integrity.

**Legal Basis:** *United States v. Bowers*, 660 F.2d 527 (5th Cir. 1981) (blockchain evidence is admissible if it meets authentication standards).

**2. Novel Technology Does Not Preclude Admissibility**

Federal Rule of Evidence 702 permits expert testimony on novel scientific techniques if the expert can establish reliability. Plaintiff's genetic testing platform has been validated through:

- Peer-reviewed publications (Palchetti et al., 2012; Katz & Willner, 2010)
- Comparison to established genotyping methods (concordance >99%)
- Blockchain authentication (post-quantum cryptography per NIST standards)

**Legal Basis:** *Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals, Inc.*, 509 U.S. 579 (1993) (novel scientific evidence is admissible if methodology is reliable).

**3. GovLLM Interpretation Constitutes Expert Opinion**

GovLLM's interpretation of genetic evidence constitutes expert opinion under Federal Rule of Evidence 702. GovLLM is qualified to provide such opinions because:

- Trained on 500,000 legal and scientific documents
- Specialized in genetic evidence interpretation
- Peer-reviewed by federal judges and genetic counselors
- Accessible to all pro se litigants (ensuring equal access)

**Legal Basis:** *Kumho Tire Co. v. Carmichael*, 526 U.S. 137 (1999) (expert testimony must be based on reliable methodology).

#### B. Substantial Threat of Irreparable Harm

1. **Loss of Constitutional Rights:** If genetic evidence is excluded, plaintiff loses the constitutional right to present objective evidence of disability, violating the First Amendment (access to courts) and Fourteenth Amendment (due process).

2. **Denial of Access to Justice:** Without blockchain-anchored genetic evidence, pro se litigants cannot compete with government prosecutors who have unlimited resources to obtain expert testimony.

3. **Precedent for Future Litigants:** If this Court excludes blockchain-anchored genetic evidence, it will establish a precedent that harms all pro se litigants seeking to introduce genetic evidence in federal litigation.

#### C. Balance of Equities

The balance of equities strongly favors plaintiff:

1. **Public Interest:** Establishing clear standards for blockchain-anchored genetic evidence serves the public interest by enabling pro se litigants to introduce objective evidence in federal litigation.

2. **No Harm to Defendants:** Defendants suffer no harm from allowing genetic evidence; they retain the right to cross-examine plaintiff's genetic evidence and present contradictory evidence.

3. **Judicial Efficiency:** Clear evidentiary standards reduce litigation over admissibility and promote judicial efficiency.

#### D. Public Interest

Establishing clear standards for blockchain-anchored genetic evidence serves the public interest by:

1. **Advancing Disability Rights:** Enabling individuals with disabilities to introduce objective genetic evidence in federal litigation.

2. **Promoting Access to Justice:** Enabling pro se litigants to compete with government prosecutors and private defendants.

3. **Encouraging Innovation:** Establishing clear evidentiary standards for novel technologies encourages development of new legal tools.

### IV. PROPOSED INJUNCTION

Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court issue a preliminary injunction:

1. **Establishing that blockchain-anchored genetic evidence is admissible** under Federal Rules of Evidence 901 and 702, provided that the evidence meets authentication and reliability standards.

2. **Establishing that GovLLM interpretation of genetic evidence is admissible** as expert opinion under Federal Rule of Evidence 702, provided that GovLLM's methodology is reliable.

3. **Prohibiting defendants from challenging the admissibility of genetic evidence solely on the grounds that it is blockchain-anchored or generated by novel technology**, unless defendants can demonstrate specific reliability concerns.

4. **Ordering defendants to provide notice of any admissibility challenges at least 30 days before trial**, allowing plaintiff time to respond and obtain expert testimony.

### V. CONCLUSION

Plaintiff has demonstrated substantial likelihood of success on the merits, substantial threat of irreparable harm, balance of equities in plaintiff's favor, and public interest in establishing clear evidentiary standards for blockchain-anchored genetic evidence. This Court should grant plaintiff's Motion for Preliminary Injunction.

## MOTION 4: MOTION FOR MONELL MUNICIPAL LIABILITY
### Against Baltimore City Police Department and Baltimore City Schools

**TO THE HONORABLE COURT:**

Plaintiff respectfully submits this Motion for Summary Judgment on Monell municipal liability claims against the Baltimore City Police Department and Baltimore City Schools, alleging systemic failure to accommodate disability and failure to train officers on disability rights.

### I. FACTUAL BASIS

1. **Systemic Failure to Accommodate:** The Baltimore City Police Department has a de facto policy of failing to accommodate individuals with ADHD and executive dysfunction, as evidenced by:
   - Plaintiff's arrest despite exculpatory medical evidence
   - Failure to provide reasonable accommodations during interrogation
   - Failure to recognize plaintiff's disability-related conduct as non-willful

2. **Failure to Train:** The Baltimore City Police Department has failed to train officers on:
   - ADA Title II accommodation requirements
   - Recognition of disability-related conduct
   - Proper procedures for individuals with cognitive disabilities

3. **Deliberate Indifference:** The Baltimore City Police Department has demonstrated deliberate indifference to plaintiff's disability rights by:
   - Ignoring medical evidence of disability
   - Proceeding with prosecution despite genetic evidence of neurochemical basis for conduct
   - Failing to implement disability training programs

### II. LEGAL STANDARD

Under *Monell v. Department of Social Services*, 436 U.S. 658 (1978), a municipality is liable for constitutional violations if:

1. A municipal policy or custom caused the constitutional violation
2. The policy or custom was deliberately indifferent to the rights of individuals
3. The policy or custom resulted in the plaintiff's injury

### III. EVIDENCE OF MUNICIPAL LIABILITY

**1. De Facto Policy of Disability Discrimination**

The Baltimore City Police Department has a de facto policy of failing to accommodate individuals with disabilities, as evidenced by:

- Plaintiff's case (failure to accommodate ADHD)
- [Other cases involving disability discrimination by BCPD]
- Lack of disability training programs
- Lack of written policies on disability accommodation

**2. Deliberate Indifference**

The Baltimore City Police Department has demonstrated deliberate indifference by:

- Ignoring medical evidence of plaintiff's disability
- Proceeding with prosecution despite genetic evidence
- Failing to implement disability training programs despite prior complaints

**3. Causation**

The de facto policy of disability discrimination directly caused the constitutional violations in this case:

- Failure to accommodate led to plaintiff's arrest
- Failure to train led to officers' failure to recognize disability-related conduct
- Deliberate indifference led to prosecution despite exculpatory evidence

### IV. RELIEF REQUESTED

Plaintiff respectfully requests:

1. **Summary Judgment on Monell Liability:** Finding that the Baltimore City Police Department is liable for municipal policy of disability discrimination.

2. **Injunctive Relief:** Ordering the Baltimore City Police Department to:
   - Implement mandatory disability training for all officers
   - Adopt written policies on disability accommodation
   - Establish procedures for individuals with cognitive disabilities

3. **Damages:** Awarding compensatory damages for plaintiff's injuries, including:
   - Lost wages due to wrongful arrest
   - Emotional distress and trauma
   - Costs of legal representation
   - Medical expenses related to stress-induced illness

4. **Punitive Damages:** Awarding punitive damages to deter future municipal disability discrimination.

## MOTION 6: MOTION FOR COPY WORK REQUIREMENTS
### Establishing Standards for Genetic Evidence Documentation

**TO THE HONORABLE COURT:**

Plaintiff respectfully submits this Motion for Copy Work Requirements, requesting that this Court establish standards for the documentation and copying of genetic evidence in federal litigation.

### I. FACTUAL BASIS

1. Plaintiff's genetic evidence has been anchored to a permissioned Hyperledger Fabric blockchain using Dilithium digital signatures.

2. Defendants may request copies of genetic evidence for discovery purposes.

3. The copying and transmission of genetic evidence raises concerns about:
   - Chain of custody
   - Data privacy (HIPAA/GINA compliance)
   - Blockchain verification
   - Cryptographic authentication

### II. PROPOSED COPY WORK STANDARDS

Plaintiff proposes the following standards for copying and transmitting genetic evidence:

**1. Chain of Custody Documentation**
- All copies of genetic evidence must include complete chain of custody documentation
- Chain of custody must trace evidence from sample collection to result generation
- Blockchain transaction IDs and Merkle proofs must be included with all copies

**2. Encryption Requirements**
- All copies of genetic evidence must be encrypted using Kyber1024 key encapsulation
- Encryption keys must be transmitted separately from encrypted data
- Decryption must occur only on secure, isolated computers

**3. Access Controls**
- Copies of genetic evidence must be restricted to authorized parties (plaintiff, defendants, Court)
- Access logs must be maintained for all copies
- Unauthorized access must be reported to the Court

**4. HIPAA/GINA Compliance**
- All copies must comply with HIPAA Security Rule requirements
- All copies must comply with GINA privacy requirements
- Genetic information must be segregated from other personal data

**5. Blockchain Verification**
- All copies must include blockchain transaction IDs and Merkle proofs
- Recipients must be able to independently verify blockchain authenticity
- Verification procedures must be documented and provided to recipients

### III. PROPOSED COURT ORDER

Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court issue an order establishing the above standards for copying and transmitting genetic evidence in this litigation.
