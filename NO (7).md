## STATEMENT OF THE CASE

Petitioner suffers from a documented neurogenetic condition involving specific Integrated Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs) at the *COMT* (rs4680) and *FKBP5* (rs1360780) loci. These biological markers, verified through fMRI and Genomic-Phenotypic Risk Scores (GPRS) developed under federal-compliant research protocols, impose a "400% metabolic biological tax" on Petitioner's executive function.

In administrative proceedings before the Social Security Administration (Cases 25-1222 & 25-1224), the SSA issued final denials by refusing to apply the "mitigating measures" doctrine of the ADAAA. Specifically, the SSA failed to recognize that the absence of the **Gov-LLM Transaction Protocol**—a $45 reasonable modification—renders the Petitioner unable to navigate the "procedural gatekeeping" of the federal justice system. The lower courts upheld these denials, ignoring the objective, scientifically validated evidence of Petitioner’s neurocognitive impairment.

---

## REASONS FOR GRANTING THE WRIT

### I. The ADAAA Mandates a Broad Construction of Disability Accommodations
The ADA Amendments Act of 2008 explicitly rejected the restrictive standards of *Sutton* and its progeny. By denying a $45 cognitive prosthetic that offsets a massive biological tax, the lower courts and the SSA have defied the Supremacy Clause and the clear mandate of Congress to ensure broad access for disabled citizens.

### II. The Failure to Incorporate GPRS into SSR 16-4p Analysis
The SSA’s reliance on outdated frameworks that ignore modern genomic science constitutes a failure to consider an "important aspect of the problem" under the APA. This case provides a vehicle for the Court to clarify how agencies must integrate objective genomic evidence into disability determinations.
