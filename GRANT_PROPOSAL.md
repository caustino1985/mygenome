## GRANT PROPOSAL: NIH BRAIN INITIATIVE + SOCIAL SECURITY INNOVATION

### Project Title: Unified Cognitive Infrastructure for Evidence-Driven Disability Determination

### Executive Summary

The proposed research program develops and validates a unified cognitive infrastructure system integrating virtualized high-density neural stimulation (VHNS-FAM) with cryptographically-verifiable adjudication pipelines (OSI-P). The system enables real-time neural state monitoring during disability adjudication, adaptive cognitive enhancement for complex evidentiary processing, and objective neurophysiological evidence generation for claimants with neurogenomic conditions.

### Specific Aims

**Aim 1:** Develop cognitive-optimized VHNS-FAM arrays achieving comprehensive brain coverage with 100,000+ virtual channels enabling simultaneous monitoring of prefrontal, motor, temporal, and parietal regions.

**Aim 2:** Implement the Unified Semantic Execution Graph (USEG) enabling cross-domain operations between neural activity patterns, evidentiary embeddings, and decision outputs.

**Aim 3:** Validate clinical safety and efficacy of cognitive-enhanced disability adjudication in a prospective cohort of administrative law judges and disability claimants.

**Aim 4:** Establish regulatory pathways for neural evidence attestation under existing evidentiary frameworks and emerging neurotechnology regulations.
